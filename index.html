University-wise CGPA to percentage formulas

Different Indian universities use different formulas to convert CGPA to percentage. Here are the most common ones:

  • VTU (Visvesvaraya Technological University): Percentage = CGPA × 10
  • Mumbai University: Percentage = (CGPA – 0.5) × 10
  • Anna University: Percentage = (CGPA – 0.75) × 10
  • JNTU: Percentage = CGPA × 10
  • AKTU / UPTU: Percentage = (CGPA – 0.5) × 10
  • Delhi University: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5
  • Most other universities (UGC default): Percentage = CGPA × 9.5

7.5 CGPA equals 71.25% using the standard UGC formula (7.5 × 9.5 = 71.25%). For VTU students it would be 75% (7.5 × 10), and for Mumbai University students it would be 70% ((7.5 – 0.5) × 10).

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is used in India on a 10-point scale. GPA (Grade Point Average) is commonly used in the US on a 4-point scale. Our calculator supports both — select "4 point (GPA)" in the scale dropdown if your university uses the 4-point system.
Each university has its own grading policy approved by their academic council. While UGC recommends CGPA × 9.5, universities like VTU and JNTU use CGPA × 10, and universities like Mumbai and Anna use a subtraction formula. Always verify with your official university marksheet or academic office.
In India, 60% and above is generally considered First Class, 75% and above is First Class with Distinction, 50–59% is Second Class, and 40–49% is Pass Class. This varies slightly by university.
